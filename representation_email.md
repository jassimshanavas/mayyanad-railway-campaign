Respected Sir/Madam,

We are writing to you on behalf of the **Mayyanad Railway Passengers’ Association**, representing over 150+ daily commuters (primarily IT professionals, office staff, and students) who travel between Mayyanad (Kollam district) and the Technopark employment hub at Kazhakuttam (Thiruvananthapuram). 

Following up on the individual representation forwarded to the Ministry of Railways in February 2026 through our local MP, Shri N.K. Premachandran, we have now consolidated our collective demand. This representation is backed by a public campaign signed by **[Number of Signatures]** regular commuters, as well as an exhaustive analysis of train running histories.

### The Problem: Commuter Distress & Lost Man-Hours
Currently, the only evening return service available for commuters heading from Kazhakuttam back to Mayyanad is **Train 56304 (TVC–QLN Passenger)**, scheduled at Mayyanad at 7:23 PM. However, this service is highly unreliable:
* **Punctuality:** Over the last 6 months, Train 56304 has kept its schedule only **36.1%** of the time.
* **Delays:** Commuters face an average daily delay of **20–27 minutes**, with a maximum delay of **68 minutes** recorded.
* **Economic Impact:** This results in an estimated **4,950 man-hours lost monthly** for Mayyanad commuters alone. 
* **Lack of Alternatives:** The road route involves a 10–16 km detour via Paravur due to the Paravur Lake, with no direct public bus link, making road transit impractical.

---

### Our Representation: Three Concrete, Operationally Feasible Asks

To resolve this issue without requiring new infrastructure or timetable overhauls, we respectfully request the inclusion of a 1-minute commercial halt for the following trains:

#### 1. Stop Train 56706 (CAPE–PUU Passenger) & Return Train 56705 at Mayyanad (MYY)
* **Rationale:** Train 56706 (reaches the area at ~6:30 PM) and its return morning service Train 56705 (reaches the area at ~7:55 AM) pass through Mayyanad but only halt at Paravur. Because Paravur and Mayyanad are physically separated by Paravur Lake, the Paravur halt does not serve Mayyanad residents.
* **Data Support:** Adding a stoppage here would allow evening commuters to reach home safely by 6:30 PM. Train 56706 has an average delay of 21.9 minutes (last 3 months), making it a much more reliable and earlier return service than the current 56304.

#### 2. Stop Train 16304 (Vanchinad Express) at Mayyanad (MYY)
* **Rationale:** Vanchinad Express departs Trivandrum Central at 5:45 PM and reaches the Mayyanad area around 6:40 PM. 
* **Data Support:** Over the last 3 months, Train 16304 maintained a 35.6% on-time record with an average delay of only 19.4 minutes. Stopping here would offer a fast and highly reliable alternative for commuters, getting them home before 7:00 PM.

#### 3. Stop Train 16342 (Guruvayur Express) at Kazhakuttam (KZK)
* **Rationale:** Train 16342 already stops at Mayyanad (6:18 PM) but skips Kazhakuttam, preventing Technopark commuters from boarding it. 
* **Data Support:** Our analysis of Train 16342 shows that it is exceptionally punctual when passing through Kazhakuttam, holding a **96.7% on-time rate** over the last 3 months with an average delay of only **2.7 minutes**. A 1-minute commercial halt at Kazhakuttam would not disrupt its schedule and would provide a highly reliable, early evening option.
