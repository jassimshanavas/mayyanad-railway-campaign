# Punctuality and Running History Analysis Report
**Prepared by:** Mayyanad Railway Passengers' Association  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Target:** Southern Railway / Ministry of Railways, Government of India  

---

## Executive Summary
This report compiles official historical running data for key train services operating between Trivandrum Central (TVC), Kazhakuttam (KZK), Mayyanad (MYY), and Kollam Jn (QLN). The objective is to present data-led evidence supporting the representation for additional train stoppages at Mayyanad and Kazhakuttam. 

Our findings indicate that the primary existing return train (56304 TVC-QLN Passenger) is highly unreliable, keeping its schedule into Mayyanad less than **36% of the time** over a 6-month window. The operational feasibility of our proposed solutions—adding brief halts to services already running on this stretch—is backed by the excellent punctuality of these services (e.g., Train 16342 passing Kazhakuttam on-time **96.7% of the time**).

## 5. Conclusion
The historical performance data clearly shows that daily commuters to/from Mayyanad are underserved by the current evening schedule. The high unreliability of the TVC-QLN Passenger (56304), combined with the lack of alternative stoppages, costs daily commuters an estimated **4,950 man-hours monthly**.

Providing stoppages for 56706/56705, 16304, and 16342 at the requested stations will distribute passenger load, improve commuter well-being, and save valuable working hours, all without requiring any changes to the existing railway timetables.
