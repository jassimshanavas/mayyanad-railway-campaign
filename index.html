Who This Affects

Hundreds of working families, every single evening

Most employees finish work at Kazhakuttam around 5:30 PM. With no earlier stoppage and a train that's late more often than not, commuters routinely reach home at or after 8:00 PM — turning a short hop into a near three-hour ordeal.

  • Reduced productivity the next working day
  • Less time with family, especially for parents of young children
  • Physical fatigue from consistently long travel days
  • No real alternative: road routes via Paravur involve a 10–16 km detour with no direct bus link

The Ask

Three concrete, operationally feasible stoppages

None of these require new trains or major timetable changes — just a one-minute halt added to services already running on this stretch.

01
56706 CAPE–PUU PASSENGER · Kanyakumari → Punalur (+ return 56705)
Stop at Mayyanad instead of only Paravur
Paravur and Mayyanad are separated by Paravur Lake with no direct bus link, so the existing halt doesn't help. A Mayyanad stop would get commuters home by ~6:30 PM (56706 avg. delay: 22m), and the morning return (56705) would reach Kazhakuttam by ~8:50 AM (avg. delay: 21m).
Saves up to 1.5 hrs/day 56706 On-Time: 33% · 56705 On-Time: 37% (3M)
02
16304 VANCHINAD EXP · Trivandrum Central → Ernakulam Jn
Add a brief halt at Mayyanad
Reaches the area around 6:47 PM. Same Paravur-Lake constraint applies, so a direct Mayyanad halt is what actually serves this catchment (avg. delay at Paravur: 19m).
Reliable, faster alternative On-Time: 35.6% (3M) · Avg Delay: 19m
03
16342 GURUVAYUR EXP · Trivandrum Central → Guruvayur
Add a halt at Kazhakuttam (already stops at Mayyanad)
This train already halts at Mayyanad around 6:18 PM — adding a Kazhakuttam stop would let Technopark employees board it directly. Feasibility is backed by its exceptional **96.7% on-time record** passing through Kazhakuttam.
No new infra needed Highly Feasible: 96.7% On-Time (3M)
